### ⚠ Issues Found

#### Issue 1: James Stevens as child of Hattie Northwood & Doug Stevens
- **Problem:** Hattie Northwood was likely born ~1875–1885 (siblings Alfred b.1873, Mame b.1880). James Stevens born ~1929 would require Hattie to be 44–54 at his birth.
- **Assessment:** Biologically very unlikely. James is almost certainly a **grandson** — son of an unrecorded child of Hattie & Doug Stevens who was likely born ~1900–1910.
- **Recommendation:** The database currently has `family-hattie-northwood-doug-stevens` with no children. The connection line on the poster likely represents a multi-generation descent, not direct parentage. An intermediate generation should be noted.

#### Issue 2: Jonathan as child of Alonzo
- **Problem:** Alonzo is listed as born ~1926. Jonathan is listed as born "1920s range." If both are from the 1920s, Jonathan cannot be Alonzo's child.
- **Assessment:** Jonathan and Alonzo are more likely **siblings** — both potentially children of an earlier-generation Jones family member.
- **Recommendation:** Re-examine the family unit `family-alonzo-partner`. Jonathan, Dorothy, and Constance may be Alonzo's siblings rather than his children. Alternatively, the birth dates may be misread.

#### Issue 3: Kimberly as child of Oliver & Diane
- **Problem:** Diane's birth is listed as ~1966. Kimberly's birth is estimated as "1960s." If both are from the 1960s, Kimberly cannot be Diane's child.
- **Assessment:** Either Kimberly was born later (1980s–1990s, which is plausible if the 1960s estimate is wrong), or Kimberly is not actually Diane's child but rather a sibling or from a different family unit.
- **Recommendation:** The confidence on both dates is VERY LOW. Mark the parent-child relationship as uncertain.
