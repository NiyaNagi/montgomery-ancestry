### Biographical Narrative

Donald M. Montgomery was born in 1912 in Detroit, Michigan. He married Millicent Betts Thompson on **May 21, 1936, in Detroit, MI** (confirmed by Millicent's obituary). The couple likely met through the Grosse Pointe / Detroit social circles where Millicent's father, Dr. Fred Eugene Thompson, practiced medicine.

Donald became a **newspaper publisher**, a career that defined the family's later life:
- **1952–1963**: Purchased and operated the **Attica Ledger Tribune** in Attica, Fountain County, Indiana. He bought the paper in January 1952 from Inez F. McDermond and C.J. VanLandeghem. He sold it in May 1963 to Fountain-Warren Publishers (headed by R.M. Hemphill) but remained as editorial manager during the transition.
- **1965–retirement**: Moved to **New Haven, Allen County, Indiana**, where he purchased and operated the **Allen County Times** and the **Moon Printing Company**.
- Millicent was co-owner of the newspapers, and together they also operated the **Petersburg Press** in Vincennes, Indiana.

Donald died on **November 19, 1990**, at age 78, at **Brighton Hall Nursing Center** in New Haven, Indiana. He predeceased his wife Millicent by 13 years.

### Biographical Narrative

Millicent was born on August 23, 1911, in Detroit, Michigan, the daughter of **Dr. Fred Eugene Thompson** and **Isabella Edna Holmes**. Her father was a homeopathic physician, a member of the Homœopathic Medical Society of the State of Michigan, a Mason, a Forester, and a Knight of the Maccabees. He married Isabella on **October 27, 1903**, in Michigan. The family lived in **Detroit, St. Clair Shores, and Grosse Pointe, Michigan**.

Millicent graduated from **Grosse Pointe High School** and earned a **B.A. from Michigan State University**. She worked briefly as a **music teacher** before marrying Donald Montgomery on May 21, 1936, in Detroit.

Together with Donald, she co-owned and operated several newspapers:
- **Petersburg Press** (Vincennes, IN)
- **Attica Ledger Tribune** (Attica, IN)
- **Allen County Times** (New Haven, IN)

She retired in 1987. After Donald's death in 1990, Millicent eventually moved to Kalamazoo, Michigan (near her son James in Portage). She was formerly of New Haven, Indiana.

Millicent was preceded in death by her husband Donald (1990), her brother Fred Thompson, and her sister Dorothy Landis.

The family had a cottage called **"Mont-Land"** at **Lake of Bays, Ontario** (Muskoka region), named by combining "Montgomery" and "Landis." The cottage was built by A.J. Chevalier, at the urging of Millicent and her sister Dorothy, who persuaded their father Dr. Fred Thompson to build it. The Northwood family (related through the Smyth/Holmes line) had earlier cottages in the same area.

### Relationship to Edward Frost Rowe — Analysis

The family photo charts show only TWO Rowe individuals:
1. **Edward Frost Rowe** (4/28/1878 – 2/9/1958), married Minnie May Smith (b. 1/12/1883)
2. **Norine Eva Rowe**, married James Eugene Montgomery

Edward Frost Rowe's **only documented child** (from the Smith-Rowe-Jones chart, Photo 6) is:
- **Irene Emma Harriet Rowe** (b. 4/17/1904), who married Clyde Sylvester Jones (6/19/1891 – 8/3/1930) in 1926

**Key analysis — Norine CANNOT be Edward's daughter:**
- Edward Frost Rowe was born in 1878 and died on 2/9/1958
- Norine Eva Rowe was born circa 1953
- While it's *theoretically* possible Edward fathered a child at age 74–75, this is extremely unlikely, especially since his only documented wife Minnie May Smith would have been ~70 years old
- Edward died when Norine would have been approximately 4–5 years old

**Most likely relationship scenarios:**
1. **Norine is a granddaughter of Edward Frost Rowe** through a SON not shown in the photos (the chart may be incomplete, showing only daughter Irene)
2. **Norine is from a different Rowe branch** — perhaps a niece, great-niece, or cousin of Edward
3. **Norine is a grandchild through Irene** — if Irene remarried after Clyde Jones's death in 1930 (she was only 26 when widowed), and had children with a different Rowe, OR if she had additional children not shown

**The connection requires further research**, particularly:
- [ ] Search for additional children of Edward Frost Rowe & Minnie May Smith (sons born ~1905–1920s)
- [ ] Search for Irene Rowe's life after 1930 (remarriage? to another Rowe?)
- [ ] Identify Norine's parents through Michigan birth/marriage records
