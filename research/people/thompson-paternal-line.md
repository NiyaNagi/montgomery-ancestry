  - **Confidence: N/A** — Further research needed in Rensselaer County church/probate records

### Children (per FamilySearch profile and cross-referenced sources)

- Possibly others not yet identified
- *Source: FamilySearch profile LHPH-R98*
- **Confidence: MEDIUM** — User-submitted family tree data; needs primary source verification

### Probate / Will
- **Will dated 1827 at Sand Lake, Rensselaer County, New York**
- Mentioned in will: **Theda C. Thompson**, **Benjamin Thompson**, **Sarah Murphy**
  - *Source: Family tradition as stated in research instructions; probate abstracts for Rensselaer County (1791–1850) available at Ancestry and county Surrogate's Office in Troy, NY*
  - **Confidence: MEDIUM** — Family tradition supported by the existence of a Calvin Thompson dying in 1827 at Sand Lake; the will itself should be retrievable from Rensselaer County probate records

### Military Service
- **Calvin Thompson (b. 1771) was NOT a Revolutionary War veteran himself** — he would have been only ~12 at war's end (1783)
- His **father** (name unknown) may have been the actual Revolutionary War veteran
- *Source: Analysis of dates; no Revolutionary War service record found for Calvin Thompson b. 1771 in DAR databases or Fold3*
- **Confidence: HIGH** (that Calvin himself did NOT serve)

### Scottish Origin
- Family tradition suggests Scottish origin
- No documentary evidence located for Scottish immigration
- **Confidence: LOW** — Family tradition only; requires ship records or naturalization documents

### ⚠ DATING CONFLICT NOTE
The research instructions describe a "son Calvin" who made a will in 1827 and a "son Samuel (b. 1767) was a snare drummer in Rev. War." If Calvin the patriarch was the *father* of Samuel (b. 1767), then the patriarch was likely born ~1740s, NOT 1771. The Calvin Thompson (1771–1827) found on FamilySearch/Find A Grave may be the **son** (who made the will), while an **earlier generation patriarch** (born ~1740s) would be the actual Revolutionary War connection. This generational confusion requires further research.

This interpretation better fits the chronology.
