   quality making the "M" appear as "B".

   not Harriette. The earlier photo's lower quality apparently obscured the initial
   letter cluster.

3. **"Charles P." vs "Charles F."**: The middle initial is clearly **P.** in this cleaner
   copy. "P" and "F" are easily confused in degraded type.

4. **"Wm. Bowes" vs "Wm. Bones"**: **Bowes** is clearly typed. This resolves Unresolved
   Question #6 from the preliminary tree. "Bowes" is a known English/Scottish surname,
   while "Bones" was flagged as suspicious.

5. **Calvin birth year discrepancy**: This document shows Calvin (Gen 3) born **1820**,
   but our validated data from Find a Grave and census records shows **1822**. The 1822
   date is more authoritative. This typed document may contain a rounding error or typo.

6. **"Burton 918"**: The year "918" is clearly a truncation of **1918** — the leading "1"
   was either cut off at the left margin of the original document or omitted by the typist.

7. **Emmaline "m." line**: Below Emmaline (1813-1895), the spouses show "J.Hill" then
   ". McGuinness" — the period before McGuinness likely represents a missing or illegible
   first initial (our DB has "L. McGuinness").

   spouse name on that line is ambiguous in alignment. Our existing data has "m. B. Bennett"
   which is not clearly visible in the spouse line of this document — the ". McGuinness"
   might be associated with Emmaline's second marriage rather than Lucinda's spouse.
